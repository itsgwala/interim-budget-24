INTRIM BUDGET

interim budget are the budget which is presented by finance miniter of sate/central. it is presented when the government tenure of five year is completed and election is coming soon. it is for short time period maximimum for three to six month.

An Overview

Presented on February 1, 2024, by Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman, the Interim Budget 2024 served as a vote-on-account due to upcoming general elections. This implies it authorized the government to spend funds for the initial four months of the 2024-25 financial year before a full budget is presented by the newly elected government

Key Highlights

* Fiscal Consolidation: The budget aimed to maintain fiscal discipline by aiming for a fiscal deficit of 4.5% in 2025-26, down from 6.4% in 2023-24.

* Increased Capital Expenditure: Allocation for capital expenditure was significantly increased to ₹11.11 lakh crore, aiming to boost infrastructure development.

* Tax Breaks: No major changes were announced in direct or indirect tax rates, although some relief measures were provided, like withdrawal of tax demands up to ₹25,000 for specific tax years.

* Focus on Agriculture & Rural Development: Schemes like PM-SVANidhi for street vendors, Lakhpati Didi for rural women empowerment, and Mudra Yojana loans for entrepreneurs received continued support.

* Focus on Social Welfare: Increased allocation for schemes like PM Jan Dhan Yojana, Ayushman Bharat, and Ujjwala Yojana aimed to support vulnerable sections.

Otential Areas for Analysis

* Impact on different sections of society: Analyze how the budget allocations and schemes benefit or disadvantage various groups, like middle class, farmers, marginalized communities, etc.

* Effectiveness of existing schemes: Evaluate the track record and efficiency of existing programs like Mudra Yojana and Skill India, identify areas for improvement.

* Sustainability of fiscal consolidation: Discuss the potential risks and benefits of aiming for a lower fiscal deficit, considering economic growth and social needs.

* Lack of major reforms: Critique the absence of bold policy changes and reforms needed to address long-term challenges like unemployment, healthcare, education, etc.

* Electoral considerations: Analyze the extent to which the budget caters to election-related concerns and populism, instead of long-term economic vision.

Reactions to the Budget

The Interim Budget received mixed reactions, with some appreciating its focus on fiscal consolidation and infrastructure development, while others criticized the lack of major reforms or significant tax changes. Here's a glimpse into different perspectives:

* Positive Reactions:

* The government and its supporters praised the budget for its stability and continuity, highlighting its contribution to economic growth and development.

* Industry experts welcomed the increased focus on infrastructure spending, expecting it to create jobs and boost economic activity.

* Critical Reactions:

* The opposition parties criticized the budget for being "election-focused" and lacking concrete measures to address critical issues like unemployment and rural distress.

* Some economists expressed concerns about the rising government debt and called for more robust fiscal consolidation plans.
